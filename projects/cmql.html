Online Geometric Defect Detection and In-situ Compensation Project

The additive manufacturing process is always characterized by geometrical variations in the printed part, such as over-fusion, lack of fusion, expansion, and contraction, all of which can compromise print quality. Therefore, determining the optimal process parameters is essential to achieving superior surface quality of the workpiece. This project aims to capture the geometric model of the workpiece during processing, enabling real-time assessment of the processing state and accuracy, as well as developing an additive manufacturing prototype with closed-loop control of process parameters.

📊 Key Contributions

  • CMQL offers remarkable potential for enhancing GH4169 machinability and near-surface integrity..
  • Tool wear decreases by ∼48% under CMQL compared with dry cutting.
  • CMQL limits subsurface hardening to ∼110 µm and induces compressive stress.
  • Simulated chip morphology and temperature-field trends generate new mechanistic insights beyond experimental observability.
