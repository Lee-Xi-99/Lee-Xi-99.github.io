Cryogenic minimum quantity lubrication (CMQL) in GH4169 machining

Nickel-based superalloy GH4169 is extensively utilized in aero-engine casings. However, its high flow strength, significant work hardening, and low thermal conductivity render machining arduous and frequently result in deteriorated surface integrity. This project intends to employ CMQL to regulate the relationship among processing, microstructure, and surface integrity during the machining of GH4169 and utilize a GPU-parallelized smoothed particle hydrodynamics thermo-mechanical cutting model to elucidate the cooling-induced alterations in the temperature field and chip formation.

📊 Key Contributions

  • CMQL offers remarkable potential for enhancing GH4169 machinability and near-surface integrity..
  • Tool wear decreases by ∼48% under CMQL compared with dry cutting.
  • CMQL limits subsurface hardening to ∼110 µm and induces compressive stress.
  • Simulated chip morphology and temperature-field trends generate new mechanistic insights beyond experimental observability.
